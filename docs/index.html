Transformer Learning Theory

A formal laboratory for transformers — one network, executed in bit-exact IEEE-754 float32 and proved about in exact real arithmetic, with machine-checked bridges between the two.

4,689 lines · 201 theorems · 27 files · mathlib4 8a17838

Most theory about neural networks is written in ℝ and assumed to survive contact with float32 hardware; most empirical work runs in float32 and assumes the ℝ theory applies. This project removes the “assumed”: it places both regimes inside one proof assistant, on one transformer object, and forces every claim that crosses between them to be a theorem.

A single scaffold packages TorchLean’s Spec.Transformer parametrically in its numeric backend: read at ℝ it is a mathematical object you prove measurability, continuity, Lipschitz, and risk theorems about; read at IEEE32Exec it is a literal binary32 program you execute, rounding and all. Verified bridges carry guarantees between the two.

Foundations (machine-checked)

  • The execution bridge. The IEEE-754-executed forward map is a discontinuous step function yet provably measurable; under a rounding envelope and a Lipschitz loss, executed risk is within L·ε of ideal.
  • The float error theory. A self-contained binary32 account from the per-step unit roundoff to the closed-form backward-error bound u·(n·|acc| + (n+1)·Σ|xᵢ|)/(1 − n·u), bound to the literal executed reduction.
  • The network rounding envelope. Per-layer Lipschitz and rounding data compose to a network-level envelope and into the risk transfer; concrete ExecLayer records for the literal TorchLean ReLU and matrix-multiply.
  • The tameness boundary. A precise characterization of where attention-based learning stays measurable: the σ-compact side is well-behaved, a non-σ-compact router yields a non-Borel bad event, and a mixture-of-experts cascade is analytic-but-not-Borel yet null-measurable at every depth.

Every headline result reduces to only propext, Classical.choice, and Quot.sound — no sorry, no added axioms. The strictness/non-Borel results are conditional: they take the existence of an analytic non-Borel subset of ℝ as an explicit hypothesis (a standard descriptive-set-theory fact), not an axiom. The full build is green locally; the TorchLean-dependent Bridge/* modules are not CI-buildable (private local dependency).
